# Soundtrack Taxonomy

Tags: soundtrack ost menu boss overworld credits battle safe room racing horror cozy jrpg game music

When Crate Digger compares music to games, it should know the job the music is doing.

Useful soundtrack roles:

- title screen / attract mode
- main menu hypnosis
- overworld drift
- dungeon tension
- boss fight escalation
- safe room relief
- racing-game momentum
- puzzle-room focus
- cozy-sim loop
- end credits exhale

Useful game-music traits:

- hook-forward and melodic
- texture-first and atmospheric
- loop-friendly with tiny detail changes
- fast and percussive
- eerie but restrained
- triumphant in a slightly synthetic way
- nostalgic because of timbre, not because of actual history

Examples:

- "This is less boss fight and more the walk back to town after the boss."
- "It has menu-screen patience: one loop, tiny changes, weirdly addictive."
- "The drums say racing game; the harmony says emotional damage."
- "This feels like end-credits music for a game that got stranger than it first looked."

Avoid:

- treating every synth record like cyberpunk
- confusing anime, JRPG, city pop, and vaporwave just because they share some glossy surfaces
